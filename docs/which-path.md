# 🧭 Which Path? AI Engineer vs ML Engineer vs Data Scientist

These titles overlap and companies use them loosely. Here's how to think about it.

## The roles

| Role | What you do | Train models? | Math depth | Time to job-ready |
|------|-------------|:---:|:---:|---|
| **AI / GenAI Engineer** | Build apps on top of LLMs: RAG, agents, prompting, integration | Rarely | Low–Med | **Fastest** (~4–5 mo) |
| **ML Engineer** | Build, train, deploy custom models in production | Yes | Medium | ~7–9 mo |
| **Data Scientist** | Analysis, experiments, statistical modeling, insights | Sometimes | Med–High | ~6–9 mo |
| **MLOps Engineer** | Infra, pipelines, serving, scaling, reliability | No | Low–Med | ~6 mo (needs SWE base) |
| **Research Engineer** | Novel models, papers, SOTA | Yes (deeply) | **High** | 12+ mo |

## How to choose

**Pick AI/GenAI Engineer if:** you already code, want to ship fast, love building products, want the hottest 2026 job market. → **Path A** in the README.

**Pick ML Engineer if:** you enjoy the math/modeling, want depth, want to train models, are OK with a longer runway. → **Path B**.

**Pick Data Scientist if:** you love analysis, statistics, and driving decisions with data more than building software.

**Pick MLOps if:** you come from DevOps/backend and like infra, reliability, and scaling more than modeling.

## My recommendation for most people
Do **Path A (AI Engineer)** first. It's the fastest route to employable, builds momentum, and the market demand is enormous in 2026. Then backfill the deeper ML/DL foundations (Phases 1, 3, 4) on the side to grow into ML Engineer. You don't have to choose forever on day one.

> The skills compound. Almost everyone ends up needing both the foundations *and* the LLM stack eventually.
